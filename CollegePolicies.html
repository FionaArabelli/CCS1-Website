COLLEGE POLICIES:

For Excused Absences:

  1. Students with severe illnesses must pass an excuse letter and a medical certificate of your illness.
  2. Students with minor illnesses must have the validity of their absence checked by the guidance counselor.
  3. Students representing the university in an event must secure an excuse letter signed by the person in charge of the said event, present it to the faculty concerned before leaving the University.
  4. For students appearing before a public or private office:
  5. Students appearing before a public or private office must secure an excuse letter with the reasons and it must be signed by the parents or your guidance counselor.
  6. Submit supporting documents from the office that requires the students' presence.
  7. Present the excuse letter to the faculty concerned.
  8. Students attending important matters at home must secure an excuse letter to be submitted to the faculty within the week the student returns.

For Make-up Examinee:

  1. It is the student's responsibility to approach the teacher.
  2. Quizzes, long examinations, hands-on exams and hands-on exercises are the teacher's prerogative.
  3. If the date of the exam is the same date a student is advised by a physician to return to class, the student may opt to postpone taking the exam.
  4. It is the faculty's discretion to set the make-up examination schedule.

For Cheating Matters:

  1. Students caught cheating in a Major Exam will result to F as their Final Grade for that Major Course.
  2. Students caught cheating in other exams or quizzes will result to F as their Grade for that particular quiz.

For All Major Exams:

  1. All students must take the major examinations on the dates set by the college.

For Prerequisite Courses:

  1. If and when a student has to take a prerequisite subject simultaneously with a subject requiring such a prerequisite, a failure in the prerequisite would mean no credit also for the subject which required it.

For Some Attendances:

  1. Students are required to attend all regular religious and academic convocations and assemblies.
  2. The first policy for some attendances also applies to all other activities sponsored by the University.
  3. Students who are absent from the foregoing activities will be subject for sanction/penalties as may be decided by the college or the Confederates Student Council.

For Laboratory Rules:

  1. Wearing of official and validated SU ID is required at all times.
  2. The facilities are for instructional and educational purposes only and students are not allowed to play with them.
  3. Students who are enrolled in any of the computer courses may be allowed to enter the lecture/laboratory rooms outside their respective class schedules only with the approval of the College or the faculty in charge.
  4. Students are cautioned against making noise that may disrupt classes in adjacent lecture rooms.
  5. Bringing of food inside the laboratory rooms is strictly prohibited except during OJT defense.
  6. Electronic devices should be turned off during lectures and exams.
  7. Using the College facilities to develop or send lewd, offensive and threatening materials is cause for disciplinary action.
  8. If found violating any of the laboratory rules, the Dean or any of his/her duty authorized representative has the option to suspend and prohibit a student from premises and more.

IRC RULES Rules To Follow:

  1. Only bona fide students, faculty and staff of CCS can borrow IRC materials for just 2 days. A validated identification will be presented at the Library Help Desk.
  2. Noise levels should be kept to a minimum. This includes the use of cellphones and music devices. Patrons are requested to turn cell phone ringers at the lowest volume or on vibrate and take phone calls in the lobby area.
  3. IRC opens at 8 AM - 12 NN and 2 PM - 5 PM, Monday-Friday. First come first served basis will be observed.
  4. Food and beverages are allowed inside the IRC but drinks should have a lid. Patrons are responsible for cleaning up. Aromatic foods are discouraged in the IRC.
